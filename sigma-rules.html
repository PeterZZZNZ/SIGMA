20 Sigma Rules a Sigma Will Follow

1. Self-Sufficiency

+

A sigma thrives on independence, solving problems without reliance on others. They prefer autonomy in work, relationships, and personal life.

2. Minimal Social Interaction

+

Unlike extroverts or introverts, sigmas engage in socializing only when necessary. They avoid unnecessary small talk and prefer meaningful conversations.

3. No Need for Validation

+

Sigmas do not seek approval from others. They make decisions based on logic, personal beliefs, and self-confidence, unaffected by societal expectations.

4. Mysterious Presence

+

Sigmas maintain an air of mystery. They reveal little about themselves and let others speculate, adding to their intrigue.

5. Deep Thinking

+

A sigma is highly introspective, often lost in thought, analyzing everything critically. They avoid impulsive actions and focus on calculated decisions.

6. Adapting to Environments

+

Sigmas are chameleons. They can blend into different social settings when required but prefer to remain on the sidelines rather than the center of attention.

7. Disregard for Hierarchies

+

They see no value in traditional authority structures. Sigmas do not submit to power unless it aligns with their own principles.

8. Embracing Change

+

Sigmas are adaptable. They welcome change and see it as an opportunity for growth and learning.

9. Focus on the Present

+

Sigmas are present-oriented. They focus on the here and now, avoiding future worries and dwelling on past regrets.

10. Embracing Imperfection

+

Sigmas accept themselves and their imperfections. They see them as part of their journey and learn from them.

11. Embracing the Unknown

+

Sigmas are open to new experiences and are not afraid of the unknown. They embrace uncertainty as a source of learning and growth.

12. Embracing the Past

+

Sigmas are not afraid to look back at their past. They learn from their experiences and use them as a foundation for their future.

13. Embracing the Future

+

Sigmas are not afraid to look forward. They see the future as a blank canvas and are excited about what they can create.
